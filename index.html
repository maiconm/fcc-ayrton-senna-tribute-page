Ayrton Senna

“Racing is about discipline and intelligence, not about who has the heavier foot. The one who drives smart will always win in the end.” ― Garth Stein, The Art of Racing in the Rain

Ayrton Senna's timeline

  • 1960 - Born in São Paulo, Brazil
  • 1973 - Senna started racing at Interlagos and entered a karting competition at the age of 13
  • 1978 - He started to contest the Karting World Championship
  • 1981 - Senna moved to England to begin single-seater racing, winning the RAC and Townsend-Thoreson Formula Ford 1600 Championships
  • 1983 - Won British Formula Three and Macau Grand Prix
  • 1984 - Scored his first World Championship point in his second race at the South African Grand Prix at Kyalami, and replicated that result two weeks later at the Belgian Grand Prix
  • 1985 - Senna was partnered in his first year at Lotus-Renault by Italian driver Elio de Angelis. At the second round of the season, the Portuguese Grand Prix, Senna took the first pole position of his Formula 1 career
  • 1988 - Senna rewrote the record books. His eight wins beat the old record of seven jointly held by Jim Clark (1963) and Prost (1984). His 13 pole positions also beat the record of nine held by Nelson Piquet (1984)
  • 1990 - Senna took a commanding lead in the championship with six wins, two-second places, and three-thirds
  • 1991 - Senna became the youngest ever three-time world champion, taking seven wins and increasing his pole position record to 60 from 127 events
  • 1992 - Won the 1992 Monaco Grand Prix in his McLaren MP4/7A
  • 1993 - Won the 1993 European Grand Prix in changing conditions, in what many considered one of the best drives of his career
  • 1994 - Senna died aged 34 after succumbing to fatal injuries sustained during his race at the San Marino Grand Prix, on 1 May 1994
Reference: Ayrton Senna's Wikipedia page
